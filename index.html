Research

I work at the intersection of analysis, geometry/topology, and scientific computation, with a focus on building mathematically structured models that remain useful in real simulations.

  • Numerical analysis and operator viewpoints
  • Topological and geometric structure in physical models
  • Machine learning for physics, with emphasis on interpretability and constraints

Projects

Beam physics simulations

Modeling, numerical studies, and analysis workflows for accelerator problems (beam dynamics, diagnostics, and uncertainty-sensitive measurements).

ML models for phonon forces

Low-rank learning for higher-order force predictions and efficient computation of derived quantities (e.g., mode-projected interactions).
