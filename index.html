About

I work at the intersection of mathematics, physics, and computation, with a focus on understanding the mathematical structures underlying physical models and their numerical approximations. My research explores functional-analytic perspectives on stability and limits in modeling, as well as topological and geometric ideas for Learning algorithms. I am passionate about developing clean scientific software and applying mathematical insights to real-world problems in sciences.

My favourite mathematician is Georg Cantor, and my favourite physicist is Emmy Noether. I am currently based in Europe and open to research collaborations and PhD opportunities.

Projects

2026-GANIL(master's thesis): Machine learning exploratory modeling of collective effects in High Energy Booster for FCC-ee. Supervisor: Dr. Adnan Ghribi, Dr. Hayg Guler.

Exploratory modeling of nonlinear optics and collective effects in the High Energy Booster (HEB) for the Future Circular Collider (FCC-ee) using machine learning techniques, mainly operator learning and surrogate modeling, with a focus on developing predictive models and simulation tools to analyze beam dynamics and optimize accelerator performance, as well as quantify the uncertainties associated with these models.

2023-IJCLaB : Resolution effects for FCC-ee Compton polarimetry. Supervisor: Dr. Aurèlien Martens.

Study of resolution effects in the context of Compton polarimetry for the Future Circular Collider (FCC-ee) by developing a numerical model and simulation framework to analyze the impact of detector resolution on polarization measurements.

2023-UNAL(bachelor's thesis): Heavy Neutral Lepton model extension for the standard model, simulation and analysis. Supervisor: Dr. Carlos Sandoval

Study of a Heavy Neutral Lepton (HNL) extension to the Standard Model of particle physics, including theoretical modeling of the production mechanisms, couplings to standard neutrinos, numerical simulation, and data analysis of potential experimental signatures in high-energy physics experiments.

2025-Sapienza: Low-Rank Decomposition for Phonon-Phonon interactions. Supervisor: Dr. Lorenzo Monacelli.

Development of a low-rank decomposition method for supervised Learning approachesto efficiently compute phonon-phonon interactions in materials, with applications to thermal conductivity and material design.

2022-UNAL: Computational Differential Geometry for integrable systems in Hamiltonian mechanics. Supervisors: Dr. Iván Contreras, Dr. Nicolás Martinez Alba

Implementation of computational tools for differential geometry to analyze integrable systems in Hamiltonian mechanics, including symplectic structures and conserved quantities.
