📄 About

I work at the intersection of mathematics, physics, and computation, with a focus on understanding the mathematical structures underlying physical models and their numerical approximations. My research explores functional-analytic perspectives on stability and limits in modeling, as well as topological and geometric ideas for learning algorithms. I am passionate about developing clean scientific software and applying mathematical insights to real-world problems in the sciences.

Favourite mathematician: Georg Cantor. Favourite physicist: Emmy Noether. Based in Europe and open to research collaborations and PhD opportunities.

🧪 2026 — GANIL (Master’s thesis)
← Back to Projects list

Keywords: beam dynamics, nonlinear optics, collective effects, operator learning, surrogate modeling, uncertainty quantification

Context. The FCC-ee High Energy Booster must balance performance with constraints from nonlinear optics and collective effects. High-fidelity studies can be expensive, motivating reduced models for exploration and sensitivity analysis.

My contribution. Exploratory machine-learning models aimed at emulating parts of the simulation pipeline, focusing on learning operators/maps rather than only pointwise regression, enabling fast scans and uncertainty-aware studies.

Methods. Surrogate modeling, operator-learning ideas, simulation-driven datasets, uncertainty-oriented evaluation.

Supervisors: Dr. Adnan Ghribi, Dr. Hayg Guler.

📌 2025 — Sapienza
← Back to Projects list

Keywords: low-rank decomposition, phonon–phonon interactions, anharmonicity, structured learning, efficiency

Context. Anharmonic phonon interactions are expensive; structure-exploiting approximations can accelerate downstream quantities in thermal transport workflows.

My contribution. Developed a low-rank representation approach and supervised-learning workflow to approximate interaction structure efficiently while maintaining physical usefulness.

Methods. Low-rank factorization, structured representations, physics-aware evaluation.

Supervisor: Dr. Lorenzo Monacelli.

📌 2023 — IJCLab
← Back to Projects list

Keywords: Compton polarimetry, detector resolution, simulation workflow, uncertainty propagation, FCC-ee

Context. Polarization measurements depend on instrument response. Resolution effects reshape observed spectra and can bias reconstructed observables if not modeled carefully.

My contribution. Built a numerical model and simulation workflow to quantify resolution effects and propagate them into polarization observables, enabling sensitivity studies and design trade-offs.

Methods. End-to-end simulation, response modeling, analysis pipelines, uncertainty propagation.

Supervisor: Dr. Aurélien Martens.

📌 2023 — UNAL (BSc thesis)
← Back to Projects list

Keywords: Heavy Neutral Leptons, beyond Standard Model, production mechanisms, couplings, simulation, analysis

Context. HNL extensions address open questions beyond the Standard Model and motivate parameter-dependent experimental signatures.

📌 2022 — UNAL
